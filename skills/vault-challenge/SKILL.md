# Vault Challenge

Pressure-test a belief, plan, or decision using the user's own vault as evidence.

## Steps

1. Identify the belief, plan, or decision to challenge (from user input).
2. Use Obsidian MCP to find vault evidence relevant to the topic:
   - Past decisions on similar topics and how they turned out
   - Contradictions between what the user says now and what they wrote before
   - Patterns of optimism or pessimism bias in past notes
   - External evidence the user has captured that cuts against the current position
3. Construct the strongest counter-argument possible.
4. Do NOT soften or hedge — the goal is to find real weaknesses.
5. End with a verdict: is this a minor concern, a major blind spot, or a dealbreaker?

## Output Format

- **The challenge**: the strongest case against the user's position
- **Vault evidence**: specific notes that support the challenge
- **What you might be missing**: the key assumption that, if wrong, breaks everything
- **Verdict**: minor / major / dealbreaker — and why
